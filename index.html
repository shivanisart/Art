No Glass Ceiling

A shared vision by Sophia Čáková and Shivani Mathur

The Beginning

Sophia Obuch Cakova and Shivani Mathur met in 2020 in what can only be described as a cathedral of art—the soaring 23-meter-high foyer at Central Saint Martins, King’s Cross, London. A space of remarkable scale and light, crowned by a ceiling of glass. And yet, to those who enter with creative purpose, there is no ceiling at all. The vision is unlimited.

Sharing the Vision

Today, we want to bring that vision to you. The freedom we enjoyed, the boundless energy we experienced, the delicate techniques we learned—and unlearned—we want to share.

This is not a traditional class. It is a conversation, a transmission, a shared moment of insight. We’re calling it “No Glass Ceiling”—because that’s what it felt like, and that’s what it still is.

About Sophia Čáková

Sophia lives between Bratislava and Croatia and frequently travels across Europe, especially to Vienna and London. A dedicated oil painter, she creates emotionally rich and technically refined work. She shares her life with her husband, her dog, and a rabbit. Sophia also tutors children and regularly holds art classes that foster skill and expression.

About Shivani Mathur

Shivani is a trader by day and a painter by night. With over 30 years in finance, she was one of the City of London’s most senior bankers. Since 2008, she has quietly pursued art, and in 2020, she began studying Art and Science at Central Saint Martins—where she met Sophia. With a background in mathematics, Shivani brings both structure and emotion to her work.

Join Us

Together, we’re opening a space for creativity—a gathering, a workshop, a studio without walls. A place to share vision, experience, and freedom. A place where there is, truly, no glass ceiling.
